Analysis of Behavioral Health Risk Factors

Correlations Between Health Risks and Age

Using data extacted from the 2014 U.S. Census American Community Survey 1-year estimates, the provided health risk factors were examined. In this graph, the percent of people who smoke versus the median age was compared by state.

The first correlation between these two factors that can be deduced is that the median age of smokers in Northeastern states such as Vermont, Maine, New Hampshire, Connecticut and Pennslyvania are over the age of 40.

The second correlation that can be deduced is that Southern states have the highest percentage of people who smoke compared to the other states surveyed. Furthur analysis is required to investigate the validity of these correlations.

Data Extracted from U.S. Census ACS 2014 Survey
